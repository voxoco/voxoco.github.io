SIP TRUNKING

Session Initiation Protocol (SIP) trunking is the use of voice over IP (VoIP) to facilitate the connection of a private branch exchange (PBX) to the Internet.

In effect, the Internet replaces the conventional telephone trunk, allowing an enterprise to communicate with fixed and mobile telephone subscribers worldwide.

Flexibility is key. It is easy to add channels to your SIP trunk to cope with increased calls. A simple phone call, or visit to your online service portal will allow you to add channels, and often this can be done immediately.

Compare that to getting additional lines installed, and then having to upgrade your old PBX to handle more lines!

BENEFITS

SIP trunking offers a host of benefits in many areas, the foremost being cost savings.

With SIP trunks you can easily choose the correct number of channels that you need, whereas with ISDN/T1 you often have to choose to add either 15 or 30 lines. This usually means you end up with expensive extra capacity. Other benefits include,

  • Lower monthly Line & DID Rental
  • Lower call charges
  • Better customer service
  • Eliminate VoIP Gateways
  • Leverage a modern IP PBX

SERVICES

SIP trunks are not bound by location, so its is easy to move offices or set up a temporary location during an annual event or in the case of an emergency.

SIP Trunking also allows you to provide better customer service by adding more geographical & international numbers. Just by adding numbers to your SIP trunk and terminating them on your IP PBX you can give customers more options to dial in, at a significantly lower cost.
